The Story

"The power of food is really spiritual. It not only brings the whole family together on the same table, but also brings the whole world together."

As generations pass one thing we can hand down is culture and tradition. There's something about mom's cooking that provides nastalgic moments and long-lasting memories. In an effort to maintain and grow tradition through the years, this recipe book will house all of our family recipes in one place. From aunts and uncles to cousins and siblings, all of our favorite/famous recipes (at least within the family) can be found here.

Butter Chicken

Marinade

  • 2lb chicken breast cut into small pieces
  • 3/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 tsp kasuri methi
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 1/2 tbs melted butter
  • 2 tbs garlic paste
  • 1 tbs ginger paste
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 3 tbs shan buttern chicken masala

Mix all ingredients and let set for 12 hours

Makhani Gravy

  • 10 garlic cloves
  • 1/2 inch ginger piec
  • 1 small stick cinnamon
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 tbs red chili powder
  • 3 tbs almond powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 3 tomatos
  • 1 onion
  • 1/2 tbs sugar
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi
  • 4 tbs oil
  • 2 tbs half and half
  • Cilantro
  • Salt and lemon juice to taste

Directions

In large skillet heat oil. Add marinated chicken and cook until chicken is no longer pink. Remove chicken from skillet and set aside. In microwave safe bowl put chopped onions, tomato ginger and garlic with little water. Microwave on high for 5 minutes. Grind mixture to paste. In skillet add clove cinnamon stick and cardamom. Stir for a minute then add the tomato onion mixture. Once the mixture starts bubbling add chicken and mix well. Add almond powder,Garam masala,sugar,salt,chili powder and kasuri methi. Stir until chicken is cooked and gravy becomes thick. Stir in lemon juice to taste.

Palak Muthiya

Ingredients

  • 1 pack frozen chopped palak
  • 1/4 cup wheat flour
  • 1 cup rava (soji)
  • 3 tsp red chilly powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 3 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp hing
  • 2 tbs oil
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • Salt to taste

Directions

Defrost palak. Mix everything together to form smooth dough. Use little bit water if needed. Make sausage shape long rolls and place it in greased container. Steam for 15 minutes on high and then lower heat to medium to steam 20 more minutes. Let Muthia cool down before cutting.

Mix Dal Puda

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup chana dal
  • 1/4 cup moong dal
  • 1/4 cup green split moong dal
  • 3/4 cup yogurt
  • 2 green chillies
  • 1 red onion chopped
  • cooking oil
  • salt to taste

Directions

Soak dals for 6-8 hours. Blend them with yogurt, chillies. Add water if needed. Add salt, coriander, turmeric. Mix properly.

Add 1tsp oil in a non stick pan. When hot, pour 1/2 cup of mixture and spread it. Add chopped onion, cover and let it cook for a few minutes then flip and cook on the other side.

Vegetable Dhokla

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Chola dal
  • 1/2 cup Channa dal
  • 1/2 cup urad dal
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 tsp eno
  • 1 cup chopped cabbage, capscicum, carrot
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup boiled corn
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger green chilli paste
  • Salt to taste

Directions

Blend the dals and rice. Add yogurt, salt and keep it aside covered for about 6 hours. Add cabbage, capsicum, carrots, corn, onion. Add eno and ginger chilli paste and stir well.

Make dhoklas. You can sprinkle black pepper on the dhokla before steaming. Let it cool slightly before cutting in pieces. Enjoy them steamed or add 1tbsp oil, mustard and sesame seeds.

Moong (Surti Style)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole green moong
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 green chilly
  • 1 small piece of ginger
  • 3 tbs lemon juice
  • 2 tbs sugar
  • 1/4 tbs turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp fenugreek for splattering
  • Chopped cilantro
  • 2 tbs oil
  • Salt to taste

Directions

Pressure cook moong wiht 2 cups of water on medium heat until two whistles. Crush garlic, ginger, and green chill. Heat pan with oil and add fenugreek seeds, cook until brown. Add crushed garlic, ginger, and chilli. Stir and add moong, lemon juice, sugar, turmeric powder and salt. Stir and cook for about five minutes until slight boil. Add chopped cilantro. Serve hot with roti.

Palak Kofta

Kofta

  • 1 cup palak leaves
  • 1 cup mashed boiled potatoes
  • Few cashew nuts chopped
  • 2 tbs grated paneer
  • 1 tbs fresh coriander leaves
  • 1/2 tsp jeera powder
  • 1-2 green chillies
  • 1 piece ginger
  • 1 tbsp Maida
  • Salt to taste

Gravy

  • 2 bunch palak
  • 2 onions
  • 2 tomatos
  • 2 tbs cashewnut paste
  • 1 tsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp jeera or cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp red chilly powder
  • 2 tsp fenugreek leaves
  • 2 tbs butter
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup cream

    • Directions

    Take all kofta ingredients and mix well. Make small round balls and roll them in cornflour then deep fry until golden brown. Boil spinach in very little water with salt. Once cooked squeeze out water and make a fine spinach paste. Add pureed tomatos and cook until oil separates. Add palak puree and cashewnut paste — mix well. Add salt, red chilly powder, garam masala, and cook for 4 minutes. Add 1/4 cup of water and let it boil. Add cream and fenugreek leaves then mix well. Finally add koftas to absorb gravy.
